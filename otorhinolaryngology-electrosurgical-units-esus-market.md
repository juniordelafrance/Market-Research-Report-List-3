<p><p>Otorhinolaryngology Electrosurgical Units (ESUs) are medical devices used in surgeries related to the ear, nose, and throat. There are three main types of ESUs: Monopole ESUs, Bipolar ESUs, and Vessel Sealing Units. Monopole ESUs use a single electrode for cutting and coagulation, while Bipolar ESUs use two electrodes for more precise surgeries. Vessel Sealing Units are specialized devices used for sealing blood vessels during surgery to minimize bleeding. These different types of ESUs cater to a variety of surgical needs in Otorhinolaryngology procedures.</p></p>
