<p><p>High precision instrumentation amplifiers are categorized into two main types based on their frequency range: Low Frequency and High Frequency. Low Frequency instrumentation amplifiers are designed to accurately measure signals that have frequencies below 1 MHz, such as temperature or pressure sensors. On the other hand, High Frequency instrumentation amplifiers are capable of measuring signals with frequencies above 1 MHz, such as bioelectric signals or radio frequencies. Each type of instrumentation amplifier is optimized for its specific frequency range to provide accurate and reliable measurements.</p></p>
