<p><p>Patient vital signs monitor market can be divided into three main types based on the acuity level of the patients they are designed for. High-acuity monitors are used for patients who require constant monitoring in critical care settings. Mid-acuity monitors are suited for patients in intermediate care units or during surgeries. Low-acuity monitors are typically used in general wards or outpatient settings for routine monitoring of stable patients. Each type of monitor is designed to meet the specific needs of patients at different levels of acuity.</p></p>
