<p><p>Junction Field-Effect Transistors (JFETs) are semiconductor devices that control current flow by varying the width of a conducting channel. There are three main types in the market: Dual N-Channel, N-Channel, and P-Channel JFETs. </p><p>Dual N-Channel JFETs have two N-type channels, allowing for bidirectional current flow. N-Channel JFETs have one N-type channel and are commonly used for switching and amplification applications. P-Channel JFETs have a P-type channel and are used in complementary circuits to N-Channel JFETs.</p></p>
