<p><p>Partial Knee Replacement (PKR) is a surgical procedure in which only the damaged part of the knee joint is replaced with a prosthetic implant, unlike total knee replacement where the entire knee joint is replaced. This procedure is commonly used for patients with arthritis affecting only one part of the knee. PKR offers several advantages over total knee replacement, including faster recovery time, smaller incisions, and preservation of healthy bone and ligaments.</p><p>The Partial Knee Replacement (PKR) Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 5.7% during the forecast period. The market growth is driven by factors such as the increasing prevalence of knee osteoarthritis, rising demand for minimally invasive surgical procedures, and advancements in implant technologies. Additionally, the growing aging population and rising awareness about the benefits of PKR among patients and healthcare providers are contributing to the market expansion.</p><p>The latest trends in the Partial Knee Replacement (PKR) Market include the development of advanced implant materials that offer improved longevity and performance, the adoption of robotic-assisted surgical techniques for more accurate implant placement, and the introduction of personalized implants designed for each patient's unique anatomy. These trends are expected to further drive market growth and enhance patient outcomes in the coming years.</p></p>
