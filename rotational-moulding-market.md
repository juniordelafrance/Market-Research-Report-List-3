<p><p>Rotational moulding, also known as rotomoulding, is a manufacturing process used to produce hollow plastic parts. It involves heating a hollow mould filled with raw material and rotating it in multiple axes to evenly coat the inside of the mould. After the material has cooled and solidified, the final product is removed from the mould.</p><p>The rotational moulding market is experiencing significant growth, with a projected CAGR of 9.3% during the forecast period. Key factors driving this growth include the increasing demand for lightweight and durable plastic products across various industries such as automotive, packaging, and construction. Additionally, the growing focus on sustainability and eco-friendly manufacturing processes is also contributing to the market expansion.</p><p>One of the latest trends in the rotational moulding market is the adoption of advanced materials and technologies to enhance product quality and efficiency. Manufacturers are investing in research and development to create innovative products with improved strength, flexibility, and resistance to environmental factors. Additionally, the rising popularity of customized and personalized products is driving the demand for rotomoulded solutions tailored to specific customer requirements.</p></p>
