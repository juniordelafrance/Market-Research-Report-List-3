<p><p>Micro supercapacitors can be categorized based on the types of electrodes they use. Some micro supercapacitors are composed of thin film electrodes made of nanostructured materials, allowing for high surface area and energy density. Others consist of in-plane interdigital electrodes, which provide fast charge and discharge rates. Three-dimensional (3D) electrodes are also used in some micro supercapacitors, offering increased surface area and electrical storage capacity. These different types of electrodes play a crucial role in determining the performance and capabilities of micro supercapacitors in various applications.</p></p>
