<p><p>Heat resistant alloys are categorized into three main types based on their primary composition: Nickel-based, Iron-based, and Cobalt-based alloys. Iron-based alloys are preferred for their cost-effectiveness and good oxidation resistance, commonly used in the automotive and marine sectors. Cobalt-based alloys are prized for their exceptional strength and wear resistance, making them a popular choice for high-stress applications in the chemical and petrochemical industries.</p></p>
